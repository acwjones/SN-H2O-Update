New PDA Functions Available

Datacam

Long-term survival beyond the bare necessities demands the survivor come to understand their environment. To facilitate this the Survival PDA now comes equipped with an onboard data capture feature. In addition to capturing standard optical information and generating images, the datacam also records actual physical information about the target for later study.

Datacam in Detail:

The datacam lens is actually a miniaturized spectroscope. It emits electromagnetic radiation in the specified direction, which bounces off or is absorbed by objects in the viewframe, and then analyses the results to determine the physical and chemical makeup of those objects.

Datacam Usage:

Datacam is currently in alpha testing stage. The optical camera is fully functional, but spectroscopic functionality will be improved substantially in future patches. For now it is best employed as a way to document your exploration and discoveries.

Data Bank

Your PDA is constantly analysing user biometrics and the immediate environment to deliver timely survival advice. Detailed reports on particular subjects can now be accessed from the Data Bank tab. This is also where you will find datacam spectroscope results, and any other kinds of data recorded in the field.

Gallery

Optical photographs generated by the datacam can be stored, viewed and edited from the new Gallery tab on the PDA menu.

Message Log

While the PDA will continue to operate in voice mode to deliver crucial information to the user, it will now also record these messages in the Message Log tab for future reference. It will also use the onboard microphone to transpose information from other sources.

Subtitles & Language Support

For users having difficulty understanding the PDA in voice mode subtitles are now available. They are displayed on the HUD, and multi-language support is constantly being improved.
